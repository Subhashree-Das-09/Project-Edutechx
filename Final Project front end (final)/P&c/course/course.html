Course Description
This introductory course in graphic design provides students with a comprehensive overview of the fundamental principles, concepts, and techniques essential to the field. Through a combination of theoretical lectures, hands-on projects, and practical exercises, students will explore the creative process and develop a solid understanding of design principles, typography, color theory, layout composition, and visual communication.
The course aims to equip students with the necessary skills to conceptualize and execute effective visual solutions across various mediums, including print and digital platforms. Emphasis will be placed on fostering creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving abilities within the context of graphic design.

Topic Covered

  1. Introduction to Graphic Design: History, evolution, and contemporary trends.
  2. Elements of Design: Understanding line, shape, form, texture, and space.
  3. Principles of Design: Exploring balance, contrast, emphasis, rhythm, and unity.
  4. Typography: Basics of type anatomy, classification, hierarchy, and typographic composition.
  5. Color Theory: Color models, harmonies, psychology of color, and color symbolism.
  6. Layout and Composition: Grid systems, visual hierarchy, and organizing elements.
  7. Image Editing: Introduction to raster graphics software and basic image manipulation techniques.
  8. Vector Graphics: Fundamentals of vector-based design software and creating scalable graphics.
  9. Branding and Identity: Principles of branding, logo design, and visual identity systems.
  10. Introduction to User Interface (UI) and User Experience (UX) Design: Basics of designing for digital interfaces.
  11. Print Production: Understanding print processes, file preparation, and output specifications.
  12. Portfolio Development: Strategies for assembling and presenting a professional design portfolio.

Course Format

  • Lectures: Theoretical sessions covering key concepts and principles.
  • Workshops: Hands-on exercises and projects to apply learned skills.
  • Critique Sessions: Constructive feedback and discussions on student work.
  • Independent Study: Assignments and readings to supplement class learning.
  • Final Project: Culminating project showcasing students' mastery of graphic design principles.

Prerequisites: None. This course is open to students with a passion for design and creativity, regardless of prior experience.

Assessment

  • Participation: Active engagement in class discussions, workshops, and critiques.
  • Assignments: Completion and quality of assigned projects and exercises.
  • Final Project: Execution, creativity, and presentation of a comprehensive design project.
  • Portfolio: Compilation of work demonstrating proficiency in graphic design principles.

By the end of the course, students will have developed a strong foundation in graphic design theory and practice, preparing them for further study or entry-level positions in the field.
